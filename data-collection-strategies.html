Data Collection Strategies

Capturing the right data is the foundation of effective search analytics.

Beyond the Search Box

To truly understand search performance, you need to look beyond the initial query. It's crucial to capture the entire user journey, from the moment they land on your site to the final conversion. This means tracking not just what users search for, but also how they interact with the results.

Key Data Points to Collect

  • User and Session IDs: To connect all of a user's actions within a single visit.
  • Search Queries: Including the original query and any refinements.
  • Clicks on Search Results: Which products are users clicking on, and in what position?
  • Filters and Facets: How are users narrowing down their search results?
  • Add-to-Cart Actions: Which products are being added to the cart directly from the search results?
  • Conversions: Did the user ultimately make a purchase after using search?

Connecting Search to Business Outcomes

In an e-commerce context, it's vital to link search behavior to tangible business outcomes. By connecting search data with transaction data, you can start to answer critical questions like, "Which search queries are driving the most revenue?" and "How does the use of filters impact average order value?"
